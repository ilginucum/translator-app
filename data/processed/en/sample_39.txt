byjulia ramirez personal detailspolitical partydemocraticeducationdepaul university baarizona state university tempe mpaloyola university chicago jd anabel abarca is an american politician who served in the chicago city council as alderperson for the th ward from to she was appointed to the position by mayor lori lightfoot and confirmed by the city council in december following the retirement of george cardenas she ran for reelection to a full term but lost to julia ramirez the th ward is on chicagos southwest side and includes portions of brighton park mckinley park and little village early life and educationedit abarca is a native of the northwest side of chicago and is the daughter of mexican immigrants she graduated from taft high school in norwood park and subsequently earned a bachelors degree from depaul university a master of public administration degree from arizona state university and a juris doctor degree from loyola university chicago school of law early legal and political careeredit abarca worked on tammy duckworths congressional campaign in and served as an aide to us representative mike quigley she later served on the boards of several nonprofit organizations from to she served as chief of staff for th ward alderman george cardenas chicago city counciledit in june cardenas announced his intention to resign later in the year after winning a primary for a seat on the cook county board of review abarca was one of four people who applied to fill the position for the remainder of the term and was selected for appointment by mayor lori lightfoot on december the city council approved her appointment in a vote on december she named public safety and city services as her top priorities upon taking office abarca was a candidate for a full term in the election and was endorsed by cardenas but lost to challenger julia ramirez personal lifeedit abaraca has lived in the mckinley park neighborhood of chicago since electoral historyedit
