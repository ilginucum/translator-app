czech jinsk pahorkatina are uplands and a geomorphological mesoregion of the czech republic it is located in the hradec krlov liberec and central bohemian regions geomorphologyedit the jin uplands are a mesoregion of the north bohemian table within the bohemian massif the uplands are rugged and sometimes have a character of flat highlands typical features of the relief are cuestas tabular plateaus horst and anticlinal ridges erosional denudation and tectonically conditioned basins and furrows the relief is complemented by several river terraces the uplands are further subdivided into the microregions of turnov uplands and blohrad uplands there are a lot of mediumhigh hills the relief is very rugged but the elevations are low the highest peaks are located in the northern part of the territory the highest peaks of the jin uplands are sokol m ft zabolky m ft dehtovsk horka m ft trosky m ft hrobka m ft vyske m ft pivina m ft musk m ft zlesk vrch m ft stelesk hra m ft geographyedit the territory is lshaped and elongated from west to east the region has an area of square kilometres sq mi and an average elevation of m ft the west of the territory is drained by the jizera river the east is drained by the elbe a notable river that crosses the centre of the territory is also the cidlina a tributaty of the elbe suitable natural conditions contributed to the creation of many settlements in the jin uplands the most populated settlements located in whole or in large part in the territory are mlad boleslav jin dvr krlov nad labem turnov mnichovo hradit hoice bakov nad jizerou and kosmonosy natureedit the territory alternates between forested and agricultural landscapes the centre and north of the jin uplands are continuously forested and are protected as the
